Day A · Exercise 1 of 6
Dumbbell Chest Press
Chest 3 × 10–12 Start: 8–10kg ▶ Watch
How to do it
Lie on the bench, feet flat on the floor. Hold a dumbbell in each hand at chest height, elbows at about 45°. Press both weights straight up until your arms are almost straight. Lower with control — 3 slow counts down. Repeat.
What you should feel
A stretch across your chest at the bottom. A squeeze in the middle of your chest at the top. Your shoulder blades pressed into the bench throughout.
What to avoid
Arching your lower back off the bench
Letting the weights drop fast — control the descent
Flaring elbows too wide — keep them at 45°

Day A · Exercise 2 of 6
Seated Cable Row
Back 3 × 12–15 Start: Light ▶ Watch
How to do it
Sit upright at the cable machine, feet on the pads, knees slightly bent. Grip the handle. Pull it toward your lower chest — lead with your elbows going straight back. At the end of the pull, squeeze your shoulder blades together hard. Hold 1 second. Let the cable return with control.
What you should feel
A squeeze between your shoulder blades at the top. Your mid-back (lats and rhomboids) doing the work — not your biceps.
What to avoid
Rounding your back to pull more weight
Using momentum — swinging back and forth
Pulling with your arms instead of your back

Day A · Exercise 3 of 6
Shoulder Press
Shoulders 3 × 10–12 Start: 6–8kg ▶ Watch
How to do it
Sit on a bench with back support. Hold dumbbells at shoulder height, palms facing forward, elbows at 90°. Press both weights up until your arms are almost fully extended. Lower slowly — 3 counts — back to shoulder height.
What you should feel
Your shoulder muscles working as you press up. A controlled burn at the top. Your core engaged to keep you stable and upright.
What to avoid
Locking your elbows fully at the top — stop just short
Shrugging your shoulders up toward your ears
Arching your lower back — keep ribs down

Day A · Exercise 4 of 6
Lat Pulldown
Back 3 × 12–15 Start: Light ▶ Watch
How to do it
Sit at the lat pulldown machine, thighs under the pad. Grip the bar wider than shoulder-width. Lean back slightly, chest up. Pull the bar down to your upper chest — think "elbows to hips". Control the return all the way up.
What you should feel
A stretch through the sides of your back (lats) at the top. A squeeze there as you pull down. Not your biceps — your back is doing the work.
What to avoid
Pulling the bar behind your neck — always to your chest
Swinging your torso back aggressively to pull
Letting the weight pull your arms up too fast on the return

Day A · Exercise 5 of 6
Bicep Curl
Biceps 3 × 12–15 Start: 5–6kg ▶ Watch
How to do it
Stand or sit holding dumbbells at your sides, palms facing forward. Pin your elbows to your sides — they stay still the whole time. Curl both weights up to shoulder height. Squeeze at the top. Lower slowly — 3 counts.
What you should feel
A hard squeeze in your biceps at the top of the curl. A stretch through them at the bottom. The slow lowering phase should feel challenging.
What to avoid
Swinging your body back to lift the weight — elbows stay still
Dropping the weight down — the lowering phase builds muscle
Going too heavy — better to use lighter weight with full control

Day A · Exercise 6 of 6
Tricep Pushdown
Triceps 3 × 12–15 Start: Light ▶ Watch
How to do it
Stand facing the cable machine, close to it. Grip the rope or bar at chest height. Tuck your elbows into your sides — they stay there throughout. Push the handle down until your arms are fully extended. Squeeze at the bottom. Return with control.
What you should feel
A hard squeeze at the back of your upper arm (triceps) at full extension. Elbows should feel like they're glued to your sides.
What to avoid
Letting your elbows drift forward or flare out
Leaning over the cable to use body weight
Using too heavy a weight — triceps are a smaller muscle

Day B · Exercise 1 of 6
Goblet Squat
Quads3 × 10–12Start: 8–12kg ▶ Watch
How to do it
Hold a kettlebell close to your chest with both hands. Feet shoulder-width apart, toes slightly turned out. Push your hips back and sit down — only as deep as is comfortable for your knee. Drive through your heels to stand back up. Keep your chest tall and proud throughout.
What you should feel
A burn through your thighs (quads) and glutes. Your heels pressing firmly into the floor. Your core keeping you stable and upright.
What to avoid
Going deeper than is pain-free in your knee
Letting your knees cave inward — push them out over your toes
Leaning too far forward with your chest
⚑ Knee Note
Only descend as far as is pain-free. If this causes knee pain, substitute with Leg Press only.

Day B · Exercise 2 of 6
Hip Thrust
Glutes3 × 12–15Start: BW ▶ Watch
How to do it
Sit on the floor with your upper back against a bench. Feet flat on the floor, hip-width apart. Drive your hips up by squeezing your glutes hard. At the top your body forms a straight line from shoulders to knees. Hold 2 seconds. Lower with control. Chin tucked, not looking at the ceiling.
What you should feel
A powerful squeeze in your glutes at the top — if you don't feel it there, squeeze harder. Your hamstrings helping. Your lower back should not be doing the work.
What to avoid
Hyperextending your lower back at the top — stop at neutral
Letting your hips drop to one side — keep them level
Pushing through your toes — drive through your heels

Day B · Exercise 3 of 6
Leg Press
Quads3 × 12–15Start: 40–60kg ▶ Watch
How to do it
Sit in the leg press machine with your back and hips flat against the pad. Place feet mid-to-high on the platform, hip-width apart. Lower the weight by bending your knees toward your chest — stop when knees reach about 90°. Press through your heels back to start. Don't lock out your knees at the top.
What you should feel
Your quads (front of thighs) and glutes doing the work. Lower back pressed into the pad the whole time.
What to avoid
Locking your knees at full extension — stop just short
Letting your lower back lift off the pad
Knees caving inward — push them apart
⚑ Knee Note
Feet higher on the plate reduces knee stress. Never lock out. This is the safest quad exercise for OA.

Day B · Exercise 4 of 6
Sumo Deadlift
Glutes3 × 10–12Start: 12–16kg ▶ Watch
How to do it
Stand with feet wider than shoulder-width, toes pointing out at 45°. Hold a dumbbell or kettlebell between your legs. Push your hips back (hinge — not a squat) while keeping your back flat. Lower the weight between your legs. Stand tall by driving your hips forward. Squeeze glutes at the top.
What you should feel
Your inner thighs (adductors), glutes, and hamstrings working. A hip hinge — not a leg bend. Your back staying flat, not rounding.
What to avoid
Rounding your lower back — flat back is non-negotiable
Squatting instead of hinging — push hips back, not knees forward
Looking sharply up — keep your neck neutral

Day B · Exercise 5 of 6
Step-Up
Quads3 × 10 eachStart: BW ▶ Watch
How to do it
Use a low box or step — below knee height. Place one foot fully on the box. Press through that heel to step up, bringing your other foot up to stand on the box. Step back down with control. Complete all reps on one leg before switching.
What you should feel
Your lead leg doing the work — quads and glutes. Your heel pressing into the box. Controlled balance on the way down.
What to avoid
Using a box higher than knee height — knee protection
Pushing off your back foot to help — lead leg does the work
Rushing — slow and controlled is better
⚑ Knee Note
Box must stay below knee height. If there is any knee pain, skip this exercise and add an extra set of Leg Press instead.

Day B · Exercise 6 of 6
Pallof Press
Core3 × 12 each sideStart: Light ▶ Watch
How to do it
Stand side-on to the cable machine. Hold the handle at your chest with both hands. Your feet are shoulder-width apart, knees slightly bent. Press your hands straight out in front of you — resist the cable pulling you sideways. Hold 2 seconds extended. Return to chest. This is anti-rotation — your whole core resists twisting.
What you should feel
A deep core tension as you resist the sideways pull. Your whole midsection bracing. This is subtle but powerful — you shouldn't feel it just in your abs.
What to avoid
Letting your torso rotate toward the cable — resist it
Holding your breath — breathe steadily
Standing too far from the cable — start close

Day C · Exercise 1 of 6
Romanian Deadlift
Hamstrings3 × 10–12Start: 10–14kg ▶ Watch
How to do it
Stand holding dumbbells in front of your thighs. Soft bend in knees — they stay in that position. Push your hips back as if closing a car door with them. Lower the weights along your legs — feel the stretch building in your hamstrings. When you can't go further without rounding your back, drive hips forward to stand.
What you should feel
A deep stretch through the back of your thighs (hamstrings) at the bottom. Your back staying flat — not rounded. Your glutes squeezing as you return to standing.
What to avoid
Bending your knees like a squat — this is a hip hinge
Rounding your lower back at any point
Going too low — only as far as your back stays flat

Day C · Exercise 2 of 6
Kettlebell Swing
Glutes3 × 12–15Start: 8–12kg ▶ Watch
How to do it
Stand with feet shoulder-width, KB on the floor in front. Hinge at hips, grab the KB. Hike it back between your legs — then snap your hips forward powerfully. The KB floats up to chest height from the hip drive. Let it swing back and repeat. Your arms guide — your hips power.
What you should feel
A powerful glute squeeze every time your hips snap forward. Your hamstrings loading on the way back. This should feel explosive — like a jump without leaving the ground.
What to avoid
Squatting the swing — this is a hinge, not a squat
Using your arms to lift the KB — hips do the work
Rounding your back on the hinge

Day C · Exercise 4 of 6
Single-Leg Hip Thrust
Glutes3 × 10 eachStart: BW ▶ Watch
How to do it
Same setup as the Hip Thrust — upper back on bench. Extend one leg out straight. Drive hips up using only the planted foot, squeezing your glute hard. Keep hips level — don't let one side drop. Hold 2 seconds at the top. Complete all reps, then switch legs.
What you should feel
A harder squeeze in the working glute than a regular hip thrust. Any imbalance between sides will become obvious — this is exactly the point.
What to avoid

Day C · Exercise 5 of 6
Good Morning
Back3 × 12–15Start: BW ▶ Watch
How to do it
Stand with feet hip-width apart, hands behind your head (or a light bar across your upper back). Soft bend in knees. Hinge forward slowly at the hips — lower your chest toward the floor while keeping your back perfectly flat. Feel your hamstrings and lower back engage. Return to standing by driving your hips forward.
What you should feel
A stretch through your hamstrings and engagement through your lower back (erector spinae). Keep the movement slow — this is not explosive.
What to avoid
Rounding your back at any point — flat back always
Going too fast — slow and controlled only
Adding weight until you have the form mastered

Day C · Exercise 6 of 6
Back Extension
Back3 × 12–15Start: BW ▶ Watch
How to do it
Position yourself on the back extension machine with the pad at your hips. Cross arms over chest. Lower your torso by hinging at the hips. Then extend back up to a neutral straight position — body forms a straight line. Squeeze your glutes at the top. Don't go beyond neutral.
What you should feel
Your lower back (erectors) and glutes working. A controlled movement — not a fast snap. Neutral at the top means your body is a straight line, not arched back.
What to avoid
Hyperextending past neutral at the top — straight line only
Going fast — slow and controlled protects your lower back

Day D · Exercise 1 of 5
Stationary Bike
Cardio20–25 minZone 2 ▶ Watch
How to do it
Set the bike to a resistance that feels comfortably challenging. Pedal at a steady pace for 20–25 minutes. Zone 2 means you can hold a full conversation without gasping. If you can't speak in sentences, slow down. This builds your aerobic engine and directly improves your insulin sensitivity — both are key for your goals.
What you should feel
Warm, working, but not breathless. Your heart rate elevated but sustainable. By the end you should feel energised, not wrecked.
What to avoid
Going so hard you can't speak — that's Zone 4, not Zone 2
Going so easy it feels like nothing — you should be working

Day D · Exercise 2 of 5
Dead Bug
Core3 × 10 eachBodyweight ▶ Watch
How to do it
Lie on your back. Arms pointing straight up. Knees bent at 90°, shins parallel to the floor. Slowly lower your right arm back toward the floor and your left leg out straight simultaneously — without letting your lower back arch. Return. Switch sides. That's one rep each side.
What you should feel
Your lower back pressed into the floor the entire time — that's the cue. Deep core tension as you resist the movement pulling your back up.
What to avoid
Letting your lower back arch off the floor — that's the whole exercise
Rushing — slow is harder and more effective

Day D · Exercise 3 of 5
Plank Hold
Core3 × 20–40sBodyweight ▶ Watch
How to do it
Forearms on the floor, elbows directly under your shoulders. Body in one straight line from head to heels. Squeeze everything — glutes, quads, core. Breathe steadily. Start at 20 seconds and build toward 40. If your form breaks, stop and rest.
What you should feel
Full body tension — not just your core. Glutes and quads engaged makes a plank much more effective and protects your lower back.
What to avoid
Hips sagging toward the floor — squeeze glutes to fix this
Hips piking up into the air
Holding your breath — steady breathing throughout

Day D · Exercise 4 of 5
Bird Dog
Core3 × 10 eachBodyweight ▶ Watch
How to do it
On hands and knees, wrists under shoulders, knees under hips. Extend your right arm forward and your left leg back simultaneously. Keep your back flat — don't rotate. Hold 2 seconds at full extension. Return slowly. Switch sides. Think "long" not "high".
What you should feel
Lower back and core stabilising to keep you level. If done right, this should feel like it's working your back as much as your abs.
What to avoid
Rotating your hips — keep them square to the floor
Lifting your leg higher than your hip — parallel to the floor is the goal
Rushing through the reps

Glutes3 × 15–20Bodyweight ▶ Watch
How to do it
Lie on your back, knees bent, feet flat on the floor hip-width apart. Drive your hips up by squeezing your glutes — not pushing through your back. Hold 2 seconds at the top. Lower with control. This is the knee-friendliest glute exercise there is.

Your glutes doing the work — not your lower back. A hard squeeze at the top. Your heels pressing into the floor.
What to avoid
Pushing through your toes — drive through your heels
Letting your lower back arch — squeeze glutes to keep it neutral
